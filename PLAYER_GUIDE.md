## Welcome

You are the leadership team of a maternity unit. Over six cycles, you'll make decisions that affect patient safety, access to care, staff wellbeing, and system resilience.

There are no perfect answers. Every decision involves trade-offs. Your goal is to build a sustainable, safe maternity service while responding to the challenges that arise.

## How the Game Works

### The Cycle Structure

Each game consists of **6 cycles**. Each cycle follows the same pattern:

1. **Briefing** - You receive information about the current situation, including any events affecting your unit
2. **Decisions** - Your team selects interventions using your available budget
3. **Results** - See how your decisions impacted the four pillars and your metrics
4. **Debrief** - Discuss what happened and why with the facilitator

## Strategy Tips

### Early Cycles (1-2)
- Establish your **baseline** - understand starting metrics
- **Invest in foundations** - training and governance pay off later
- Don't overspend on one pillar at the expense of others

### Mid Cycles (3-4)
- **Respond to patterns** - what's working, what's not?
- Watch for **compounding problems** - small issues grow
- **Balance short and long term** - immediate fixes vs sustainability

### Late Cycles (5-6)
- **Address your weaknesses** - what's dragging you down?
- Consider **recovery actions** if something has spiralled
- Think about **sustainability** - could your service continue like this?

### General Advice

1. **No single pillar wins** - balance is important
2. **Staff problems cascade** - tired staff make mistakes and leave
3. **Backlog compounds** - delays create more delays
4. **Invest in prevention** - it's cheaper than crisis response
5. **Read the events** - they give clues about what to prioritise
6. **Discuss as a team** - different perspectives help

## Discussion Questions

During debrief, consider:

- What trade-offs did we make? Were they the right ones?
- What surprised us about the results?
- Where did a small decision have a big impact?
- What would we do differently if we played again?
- How does this mirror real-world challenges?
